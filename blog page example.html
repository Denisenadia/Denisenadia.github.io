2021 TOP 5 Low-Budget Beach Destinations

 

1. Sunny Beach, Bulgaria 

A recent study reveals that prices in the Black Sea Resort of Sunny Beach are at least a third cheaper than most places in Europe, especially for all-inclusive hotels. The local prices dropped by 10.7% since 2018, visitors can purchase a bottle of local lager for just £0.70, while a two-course lunch for two people (without drinks) costs £8.42.  

2. Marmaris, Turkey 

Marmaris is well known for its white, sandy beaches like Icmeler Beach, as well as its beautiful marina. If you stay away from the main strip of the Marmaris Marina and head further onto the alleys, you will find some great bargains, with a pint of local lager starting from £1.40 and a three-course dinner for two from as little as £20. 

3. Costa del Sol, Spain 

Spain has always been a hit for brits, and places like Torremolinos in Costa del Sol offer a wide range of great deals for couples and families. If you find yourself wondering around the beautiful coastline and arrive in Marbella, if you are not looking to spend much, stick to only walking around this place, as it is on the pricey side of the coast. 

4. Crete, Greece 

 We all remember the holiday to Malia from The Inbetweeners, right? Well, if you want to visit this beautiful island, you can do it while still getting good value for your money, and you do not have to be a nightlife person necessarily, as the island is so big, there are quieter areas, and a lot of those areas are catered for families. If you don’t stay in an all-inclusive hotel, head to places where they sell street food to get yourself some cheap, but absolutely delicious foods.

5. Malta 

Who doesn’t like a place where one of the official languages is English, where is extremely hot, gorgeous scenery and affordable? Malta is definitely an excellent choice, and is more than likely that this country is going to be on the UK’s green list, making traveling possible as early as the beginning of June. Be aware that there are not many sandy beaches in Malta, most of them have stones and rocks, however you can visit the surreal Blue Lagoon to make it up for it. Also, if you want to stay in a modern-looking hotel, these could be quite expensive. Most hotels in Malta are pretty outdated, but their cleanliness cannot be doubt.
